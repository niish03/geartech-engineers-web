    PRODUCT RANGE

    • Bevel gears

      Bevel gears are most commonly used to transmit power between shafts that intersect at a 90 degree angle.

    • Spiral bevel gears

      Hypoid gears look very much like a spiral bevel gear but they operate on shafts which do not intersect, which is the case with a spiral bevel gear.

    • Spur gears

      Spur gears transmit power through shafts that are parallel. The teeth of the spur gears are parallel to the shaft axis.

    • Worm gears

      Worm gears transmit power through right angles on non-intersecting shafts.

    • Helical gears

      Helical gears have teeth that are oriented at an angle to the shaft, unlike spur gears which are parallel.

    • Double helical gears

      Double helical gears are a variation of helical gears in which two helical faces are placed next to each other with a gap separating them.

    • Double shaft gears

      Herringbone gears are very similar to the double helical gear, but they do not have a gap separating the two helical faces.

    • Internal gears

      Internal gear assemblies are more compact than external gear assemblies. Internal gears are primarily used for planetary gear drives.

    • Combined gears

      Combined gears transmit power through one or more of the gear axes

    • Time pulley gears

      Time pulley gears transmit power through right angles on non-intersecting shafts.
